Dragon's Dogma 2 offers much the same as the previous installment. Expect varied combat and gameplay options, a graphically fine presentation, beautiful soundtrack and a world that demands to be discovered. At the same time, it is unfortunate that the story is somewhat simple and short, the performance occasionally disappoints and the variety of enemies is somewhat limited. Points to consider, but that does not take away from the fact that the sequel as a total package is a must have for fans of the franchise.

In case it hasn’t been made abundantly clear by now, Tekken 8 is a must-play for fans of the series, fans of the genre, or even for new players wondering what all the hype is about. It’s a standout entry in a phenomenal series, it respects the player’s time, and gives them a ton to do, and it’s a joy to play.

"Skull and Bones delivers a catastrophic disappointment. Ubisoft’s attempt at crafting a pirate-themed adventure collapses under the weight of its own mediocrity. The game promises exhilarating naval combat and a vibrant open world, but instead, it offers a shallow and repetitive experience marred by technical issues and poor design.

The original Dead Rising shows its age these days but it’s still a fantastic experience for those who can adapt to it. Deluxe Remaster addresses this, adding a whole host of small additions, quality of life improvements and a beautiful graphical overhaul that keeps the core of the game intact. As much as I did miss the original voice cast, Deluxe Remaster cements itself as the way to play Dead Rising.

EA Sports College Football 25 delivers a dream for college football fans with its extensive licenses and authentic gameplay. The game excels in capturing the spirit of college sports, but for those who aren’t die-hard fans, the experience might feel too niche. It’s a celebration of college football, but its appeal may be limited to those who live and breathe the sport.
